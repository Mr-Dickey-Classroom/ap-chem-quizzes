Setup Before You Begin

Open the Walter Fendt simulation at the link above. You will adjust three parameters: Wavelength (color of light in nm), Spacing between slits (distance d in nm), and Angle (direction from center). The simulation shows the 3D wave pattern (top) and the interference pattern on a screen (bottom strip). Use the Maxima and Minima dropdowns to locate specific bright and dark fringes.

d · sin α = k · λ     (maxima)       y = λL / d     (fringe spacing)
d = slit spacing (nm) α = angle to fringe (°) k = order (0, 1, 2…) λ = wavelength (nm) L = slit-to-screen distance y = fringe spacing on screen
Part 1 Exploration — Getting Oriented

Set the simulation to default values: Wavelength = 600 nm, Spacing = 1000 nm. Click Interference pattern to show the screen view. Take a minute to explore before answering.

1 The bottom strip shows the interference pattern. Describe what you see — how many bright bands are visible? Are they evenly spaced? What color are they?
2 The simulation shows "Maxima: 0.0° (k = 0)." What do you think k = 0 means? Where is this fringe located in the pattern?
3 Switch to Intensity profile. How does this view differ from the interference pattern? What does the height of each peak represent physically?

Analysis Conclusion Questions
