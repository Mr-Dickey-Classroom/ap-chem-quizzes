📖 Quick Reference — Concepts You'll Use

CED 5.1.A — Reaction Rates

The rate of reaction measures how fast reactant concentrations decrease (or product concentrations increase) per unit time.

For:   aA + bB → cC + dD rate = −(1/a)Δ[A]/Δt = −(1/b)Δ[B]/Δt = (1/c)Δ[C]/Δt Rates are positive values; stoichiometric coefficients normalize them.

CED 5.2.A — Rate Law

The rate law is determined experimentally — you cannot read it from the balanced equation alone.

rate = k[A]ᵐ[B]ⁿ k = rate constant (temp-dependent)
m, n = reaction orders (found from data)
overall order = m + n

Finding orders from initial rate data:

Compare two experiments where only ONE reactant changes: rate₂/rate₁ = ([A]₂/[A]₁)ᵐ Solve for m by inspection or logarithms.
