Key Vocabulary

Normal line — an imaginary line perpendicular (90°) to a mirror surface at the point where light hits it.
Angle of incidence (θᵢ) — angle between incoming ray and the normal.
Angle of reflection (θᵣ) — angle between outgoing (reflected) ray and the normal.
Focal point (F) — the point where parallel rays converge after reflecting off a curved mirror.

Real-World Connection
🔦
Flashlight / Headlight
Object placed at focal point → parallel reflected beam. Makes directed light.
🔭
Reflecting Telescope
Very distant objects → image forms at focal point. Collects light from stars.
💄
Makeup / Shaving Mirror
Object inside F → magnified upright virtual image. Face appears larger.
